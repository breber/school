Written Reflection

When one thinks of a typical English class, they normally think about reading books and writing papers. Written communication has historically been one of the most prominant forms of communication. In English 250H PB we wrote two main papers, and worked in groups to write reflections on in-class activites. I feel that I have learned a lot about myself as a writer, and I think that I am able to improve on my writing by taking this new knowledge and applying it to my typical day's writings.

Writing is not something that I enjoy doing, but I think that if I put my mind to it, my final product will be great. The type of writing we did this semester was more interesting to me than the writing I remember doing in high school English classes. This semester we were able to write about an interesting place of our choice. Being able to use multiple forms of communication (written, and visual being the main two) made the experience more enjoyable. The other major paper we wrote was analyzing a documentary. I watched Supersize Me, a movie I had seen parts of before. Getting the chance to watch it fully and with more concentration focused on it was a great learning experience.

Some of the things I noticed about my writing this semester is that I used the same sentence structure a lot. My writing wasn't varied much. This makes the writing less interesting for the reader, therefore lessening the communication that occurs. If you look at my revision of my place paper, you will notice that I tried to eliminate unnecessary sections, vary my sentence structure, and add more of a counter-argument that will therefore strengthen my original argument.
