Visual Reflection

I have always thought pictures and photography were interesting. When I was younger my sister was really into figure skating, and I would go to most of her competitions and help my parents take pictures. I really enjoyed doing that. Also, in my marching band it was tradition for each section to have its own t-shirt. My junior and senior year I designed them with some help from others. To do this, I used photoshop to edit some pictures and add effects and get the colors down enough so that it would be inexpensive enough to put on a shirt. I really enjoyed that as well.

When I was working on the visual baseline assignment, I had many thoughts about what I wanted to photograph, and how to do it. I didn't have much time, so I figured that I would be using the camera on my phone. I knew that it was able to take quality pictures at a resolution of up to 1280x960. My only problem was that the camera on the phone only worked well in decently lit situations, which eliminated my ideas about dawn pictures. Later I found the monument for the first computer bug on the west side of Coover, and decided to take a picture of it in the morning. I am pretty pleased with how that turned out.

To the left is one of my postcards that we made to learn about Photoshop. I used a radial background gradient to surround the apple, and I played around with the 3D transformations that Photoshop CS4 introduced. I think that it was a good way to introduce us to Photoshop, but I am still not quite pleased with the design of this postcard.

Later when we were told to design a poster using pictures and information, I figured that it wouldn't be too difficult to use Photoshop. I knew that technically I could put this poster together, but I wasn't sure what sort of design I wanted. I am not very creative, so designing the poster was the difficult part and putting it together in Photoshop was the easy part.

Through this class, I have learned a lot about what is visually effective, and this helped in the design process. Instead of randomly placing pictures around the page, I used a basic grid pattern, with radial motion depicted. The title was big, but I think that was a good choice because it easily catches the eye. Comparing my first and final drafts is an eye-opening experience. There are many things that I am glad I changed for my final draft because it looks much more professional and pleasing.
