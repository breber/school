Multimodal Reflection

In today's society, it is almost impossible to create a piece that is strictly one mode. Almost all of our projects could be considered multimodal, but the one that is the clear winner is the ePortfolio itself. Before this class, I didn't really think about how I received information. I now realize that there are four main types: written, oral, visual, and electronic. I also learned that these can be combined to make communication even more effective. Although in some cases I think using too many modes can decrease effectiveness and become distracting. For example, if on this page I had a video set to automatically play, background music playing, lots of colors scattering the page, and words in different sizes and fonts, this would become very distracting. This is where a minimalist approach may work best.

Throughout this class, I have come to notice the importance of using multiple modes. Let me bring you back to the example I used in another reflection, a teacher's powerpoint presentation. If a teacher just stood still in front of the class and used no visual aides, and talked in a monotone voice, would you find it interesting? I think that more than half the class would be sleeping. Although if they use some visual aides, such as a powerpoint, or even a sample experiment, more of the class will be awake.

This ePortfolio has taken a long time to create, and this is because I need to connect all of the different projects together. Multiple modes were used in different projects, and putting them all together and analyzing how I put them together is no easy task. While it may not be easy, it is a great learning experience and I feel that it has made me look deeper into the work that I did, therefore making me understand how I do things better. This understanding is what will make me a better communicator later in life.
