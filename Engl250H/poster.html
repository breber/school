Poster Project

Poster Revisions

This is one of the first designs of my poster. I had some drafts that preceded this one, but this is what I brought in to my conference with Professor Payne. Looking back on this version, there are a lot of design issues that are distracting. One of the main problems is the dark lettering in the title overlaying the dark background. This is pretty difficult to read. To fix this I lowered the opacity on the background, and inverted the colors on the letters. This alone made it look much neater.

In addition to the title, I added a couple more statistics in the right box, and then adjusted the spacing so that it wasn't as cramped. I aligned the bottom-center picture with the rest of the bottom boxes. There were a lot of minor changes, which all led up to the final draft. These many small changes made a huge difference.

Rhetorical Analysis of Visual Communication

The purpose of this poster is to communicate information about the Youth in Music Champoinships. What makes this poster successful is its use of the different rhetorical techniques that make objects visually attractive. One technique used is a grid. There are six main sections of this poster. Each section contains either an image or text. This makes it easy for the viewer to group similar information and this leads to the directionality provided in the poster. This poster has rotational directionality originating at the bottom-center. The pictures on top are rotated to match the angle from the bottom-center image.

This poster also contains a contrast between the background and foreground. In the background, there is a faded image of a ground of students marching. In the foreground, we see the prominent images, as well as all of the text. I felt that having an image in the background would make more sense than having a solid color. By lowering the opacity we are able to still see it, but it is not overpowering.

Also, the colors that are used throughout the poster are similar, and are kept to a few select colors so as not to be distracting. I use the grayish background color in the text boxes, and then the text is the purple that is used in the color guard uniform. In the first section on this page, I talked about my choices in the color of the title text.
