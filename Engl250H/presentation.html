Oral Presentation

Preparation

When I thought about this presentation, I had mixed feelings. Really, this presentation was not supposed to be that long; only about three minutes long. While three minutes may feel like a long time when presenting, it really isn't. I started planning my presentation, and figured I had plenty of information. When I went in for my conference, I had some general ideas of what I wanted to talk about, and then after I talked with Professor Payne and cleared up my ideas, I was able to create an outline.

After I created that outline, I didn't look at it until the day before I presented. Then I looked at it, went through it in my head a bunch of times, and felt I was ready. What made this presentation easy for me was that I knew the event well, and could talk about it easily.

Rhetorical Analysis of the Presentation

I think that this presentation is much better than my baseline presentation in terms of rhetorical structure. There is much more substance to it, and the organization is much more logical. The reason for this is that I prepared more than I did for the baseline presentation and I knew what I was going to say. I put a lot more thought into it, and this helped tremendously. Not only with the content, but also with my nerves. I felt much more relaxed in the final presentation than I did with the baseline.

In terms of rhetorical analysis, my presentation was organized in a chronological order. This is what made the most sense with my event. I started out at the beginning of the day, and followed the Rosemount High School Marching Band around as they warmed up and performed. This chronological organization not only made sense, it also made it much easier to remember and recite.

In this presentation, I used both verbal and nonverbal delivery techniques. This combination allowed there to be some variety, and made it less like a lecture and more informal. I felt that it was very informal espeically because it was in front of a small group, which made it easier to connect with the audience. Also the use of gestures such as pointing to certain parts of the poster helped engage the audience.
