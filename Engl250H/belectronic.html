Electronic Baseline

The Making of the Video

I didn't think this assignment would be that difficult, because I greatly enjoy using technology. I found it to be somewhat difficult actually. First off, I didn't have a decent video camera. I tried taking video on my cell phone, but that video was of poor quality. I then decided to take still pictures and stitch them together like a slideshow, and add some background music. This was the extent of my difficulties with the technology aspect.

I had a pretty good idea of what I wanted to film/depict. I knew of some great places around campus, and I had some pretty good pictures of these places. My next problem was that I had too many pictures to fit in a 30 second time slot, so I had to go through and choose the best pictures for the final cut. So the content wasn't the hard part, it was just trying to put it together in a good way.

Rhetorical Analysis

Being that this video could only be about 30 seconds, it was difficult to incorporate a lot of the rhetorical devices mentioned in the student guide. In any case, I will tell you about the devices that were used. Firstly, the whole movie can be considered a database of my experiences in my first week or two here at Iowa State. This database helps classify my experiences by what was important enough for me to not only take a picture, but to also include it. As I said a few paragraphs ago, I had more pictures than I had time for in the video, and therefore the ones included are the most important.

Another technique used in this video is layering. There are both visual layers as well as audio layers. All of the visual objects are layered on top of the music I selected. Having a video with no sounds or no visual elements would not be very effective. So I chose a song that I felt embodied my feelings, and added the most important pictures on top of it. In addition to the visuals layered on top of the audio, there are also visual layers on top of other visual layers. For instance the text that is layered on top of the pictures. The order of the layers makes a big difference. For example, having the picture on top of the words would be very ineffective given that the words would not be seen.

Another important element is the transformations. The pictures transform in and out of view, making it more interesting for the viewer. I think that transformations in and out are important. One example that I can think of right away is powerpoint presentations. I have noticed that teachers' powerpoints are much more interesting when there are transformations in them instead of the default slide to slide effect. This is why I decided to use them.
