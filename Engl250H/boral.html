Oral Baseline

This short, 2-minute presentation was our first assignment in the class. We presented on the second day of classes, in front of an audience we didn't know at all. This experience was a helpful experience. I felt that presenting to an audience I didn't know was partly comforting, but also scary. They didn't know who I was, so this would be their first impression of me; I didn't want to make a fool of myself on the second day of classes. On the other hand, I think that I was able to talk about an experience without the audience having a preconceived notion of me and my topic.

When I first started thinking about the topics I might want to do my speech about, I wasn't quite sure what I wanted to talk about. I have had a few objects or experiences that have been important in my life. Most of the options I was considering dealt with band; Bands of America Super Regional competition with my marching band, the trip to China with my band, or even my trumpet itself. Eventually I decided to combine all those ideas, and talk about the whole band experience and how it has influenced me. I am glad I chose to do this. I feel that the overall band experience is what influenced me the most.

I started jotting down different ideas of what I may want to talk about dealing with band. I came up with a pretty good list, but I didn't really know how long each would take to talk about, so I came up with a few more just to be safe. In the end, I had more than I needed to talk about, which is a good thing. I didn't actually rehearse what I was going to say, mainly because I felt I knew the topic really well, so it wasn't necessary to plan out exactly what I wanted to say.

I was feeling pretty nervous the morning of and when I was sitting there waiting for my turn. When it was my turn and I went up to the front, the time seemed to fly by. I looked up and saw the 30 second card, and I still had things I could say. I wasn't really nervous when I was actually giving the speech. It is more the anticipation that was making me nervous.

Overall, I think it went pretty well. I probably could have done better with making my sentences flow together, but I think that was because I was nervous and speaking to an audience that I didn't know.
