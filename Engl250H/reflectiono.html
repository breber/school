Oral Reflection

As with the majority of the population, public speaking is definitely not one of my favorite things to do. As with writing, I think that if I try to eliminate my nervousness, I can do a pretty good job at it. I have noticed a drastic change in my public speaking abilities in this class. I have a few ideas as to what may have influenced the change.

First of all, let me talk a little bit about my speaking habits. I have been told that I have a great speaking voice, even though I am a fairly shy person. When I speak I get nervous, but I don't think my voice really shows I am nervous. What I really notice when I am speaking is that my legs shake, and I will oftentimes sway to keep myself from putting too much stress on one leg. Another tendency I have is that I don't really know what to do with my hands. I would like to keep them in my pockets, but this probably isn't appropriate for a speech. Professor Payne suggests to use gestures, but this doesn't really come naturally to me. Although it doesn't necessarily come naturally, I noticed when I do it, I am able to remember more and the speech comes more naturally.

My baseline speech was not a very good speech, and I guess that is to be expected. But I noticed myself becoming a lot more comforatble with my small group, and sharing with them was a lot easier. In the full class setting I still noticed that it was easier to present because I felt I was getting to know them more. I think this was the major piece that made my poster presentation go well. Also, presenting in to the smaller groups in a somewhat informal setting also helped.

I am not the type of person that can make things up on the spot - at least not very well. This means that I need to write down my ideas, and practice it. While I don't like practicing, I know that it makes my speeches more coherent. For the baseline speech I didn't really practice at all. On the other hand, for the poster presentation I did more practicing - even going through it in my head the night before helped a lot.

I feel that I have grown as a speaker throughout this class. This doesn't mean that I like it any more than I did entering the class. It just means that I am able to feel a bit more comfortable doing it, and this itself will help tremendously.
