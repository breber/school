Place Paper

About the Assignment

One of our first assignments was to write a paper about a place. I decided to write my paper about the Parks Library. To do this paper, we needed to visit and document the place we chose to research. I went to the library one afternoon, and collected a bunch of pictures that I thought did a good job at portraying the unique atmosphere that the library provides. I also tried to get some pictures that show the interesting and appealing arcitecture of the building. Here is a collection of pictures that I used for my place paper.

The Revision

This paper was our first major paper of the semester, and I don't think it was one of my better papers. This is why I decided to revise it. I looked at the comments Professor Payne gave me, and I went through and made those changes. Along with that, I added a couple of paragraphs that added a better counter-argument so that my initial argument would therefore be strengthened.

There were a lot of little things that I changed in my revision. Some of them included mechanical/technical changes such as changing the title to a Sans-Serif font, and adding captions. In my previous edition I had captions on my pictures, but they only seemed to work on Windows. So this time I figured out how to add the captions on my Mac. Another change I implemented was changing the pace of my writing. In the original draft it was too slow; I spelled out too many obvious ideas. In the revision I took out the obvious statements, and revised it to have varying sentence lengths and styles.

Rhetorical Analysis

In making this paper, I chose a thesis statement that I felt I could back up and feel comfortable writing about. The thesis is an important part of the essay, and making sure that you believe in it is vital in creating a well-written paper. If you don't belive in your thesis, your writing will sound uninterested and the reader will be able to tell. In addition to the thesis, I tried to vary my sentences to keep interest high in the paper. This is one of the many things I added in my revision. I now understand that the variation helps keep the reader interested, which is important.

The document is organized in such a way that the paragraphs are pretty evenly spaced, and the pictures are placed at logical points in the paragraph. I also tried to put them on different sides of the paper so that it wouldn't look like one side was more picture-heavy than another. This is one thing that bothers me a lot is when spacing isn't correct, or the spacing tends towards one side more than another. So I was sure to be careful with the placement of the images.
